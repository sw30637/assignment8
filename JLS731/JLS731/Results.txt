It appears that the overall distribution shifted down/left (i.e. less income per capita) during the "Great Recession" and in the aftermath. However, as the global economy started to recovery in 2011/2012, income per capita started to rebound. Looking at the region specific charts, I noticed that the "Great Recession" didn't hit each region equally or in the same direction. For most regions, the "Great Recession lowered income per capita. However, it appears that Oceania didn't see a drastic decline income during the Great Recession but later on the in recovery (e.g. 2012, the distribution narrowed and declined. Also, some countries in Asia did particularly well during the "Great Recession".
